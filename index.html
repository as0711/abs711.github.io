Complexity of human activities

Human bodies are capable of generating a diverse set of movements. We have some intuitions about which activites are more complex than the others, for example walking forward in a straight line might be considered less complex than navigative obstacles. Then, there are mathematical notions of complexity for example dimensionality, variability etc. In this work, we explore whether different measures of complexity agree with our intuitions.
